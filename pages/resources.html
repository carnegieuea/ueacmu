A Step-By-Step Guide for CMU Entrepreneurs

We know being an entrepreneur can be stressful. That's why we are here to help :)

The following is a step-by-step guide we put together to help you navigate the entrepreneurial resources on the CMU campus..

If you are looking for more resources or want to add additional resources to this page, feel free to contact us.

Step 1: Share your ideas with others

If you have an awesome idea, great! Trying to be super protective of your ideas? Not so great.

Sharing your ideas can allow others to connect valuable resources to you and provide you with feedback. Some people you may consider reaching out to are the board members of UEA as well as the Innovation Scholars from CMU's Swartz Center for Entrepreneurship

These people are more than happy to help you. Try to ask them out for a coffee chat before going any further!

Step 2: Go to the Swartz Center!

Now that you have talked to several fellow entrepreneurs about your idea, you feel comfortable to move forward with your project. Then it's time to go to the Swartz Center for Entrepreneurship ! Try to email them and tell them about your idea.

The faculty in Swartz will most likely schedule a meeting with you. If the meeting goes well, you will be accepted to the Project Olympus which is an incubator program here at CMU. Congratulations! You just kick-started your company!

Step 3: Go to the CONNECTS

As an entrepreneur, you will want to meet with like-minded people and learn more about the entrepreneurial resources in Pittsburgh. CONNECTS seminar series is a good place to go to.

It is tailored mainly for students to help take their ideas from conception to commercialization, the workshops run throughout the academic year. Past seminars include: How to Find and Evaluate Your Startup Ideas; Online Marketing for Startups; and check out their website for more info!

Even if you haven't started your company yet, you are welcome to join!

Step 4: Join Pitch Competitions!

Your project is on the track right now. It's time to raise some funds. As an undergraduate student, your the best way to raise funds may be joining pitch competitions.

In pitch competitions, you normally have 3-6 mins to pitch your idea to a panel of investors. Some of the best pitch competitions for undergrads on campus are our very own CMU Venture Challenge and the McGinnis Venture Competition .

In our CMU VC, you will compete with other undergrad start-ups across the country, whereas in McGinnis Venture Competition you compete with other CMU teams including maters and PhDs. Definitely join both if you want to gain more experience!
